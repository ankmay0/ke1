III.02Railway formation & rehabilitation

RDSO-grade formation works, end-to-end.

End-to-end execution of railway formation construction and rehabilitation works in accordance with RDSO specifications (RDSO/2020/GE: IRS-0004) and the Indian Railways Construction Manual — for safety, performance and long-term durability.

Authorised applicator · geocell, geogrid & geosynthetic ground-improvement systems
01

Track dismantling

Systematic removal of existing railway tracks, rails and sleepers with proper handling and safe material management procedures.

02

Ballast & subgrade excavation

Excavation and removal of ballast, gravel and weak subgrade materials using specialised heavy equipment and controlled methodologies.

03

Ground improvement

Soil stabilisation and embankment strengthening using geocell, geogrid and engineered compaction techniques for improved load-bearing capacity.

04

Formation construction

Execution of new railway formations with layer-by-layer filling, drainage provision and compaction as per RDSO guidelines.

05

Ballast spreading

Spreading and profiling of ballast layers along the prepared railway formation for stable track support.

06

Track bed & P-way

Sleeper laying, rail linking, track alignment, packing and final track-bed completion works.

IV.02Safety & quality assurance

Safety. Quality. Compliance.

Engineering infrastructure that meets the highest operational and regulatory standards.

/01 RDSO compliance

All railway formation and rehabilitation activities are executed in strict compliance with Research Designs and Standards Organisation (RDSO) specifications and Indian Railways Construction Manual guidelines — ensuring operational safety, structural integrity and regulatory adherence.

/02 Safety management protocol

Comprehensive safety measures are implemented across all project phases — proper site signaling, workforce safety training, usage of certified personal protective equipment (PPE), and emergency response preparedness during block and operational activities.

/03 Quality control & assurance

A multi-level quality assurance system is followed through every stage of construction — material testing, compaction verification, dimensional inspections and detailed documentation to maintain high engineering standards and long-term durability.

/04 Environmental & regulatory compliance

Projects are carried out with a strong commitment to environmental sustainability — effective dust suppression, noise management, waste disposal practices and protection of surrounding ecological systems in accordance with applicable environmental regulations.
